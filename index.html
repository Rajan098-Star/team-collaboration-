Welcome to PUNJAB

Punjab, meaning "Land of Five Rivers," is a culturally and historically rich state in northern India. Punjab's map outlines its geographical location in northern India, bordering Pakistan to the west, Jammu & Kashmir and Himachal Pradesh to the north, Haryana to the south, and Rajasthan to the southwest. The state is divided into 23 districts, with Chandigarh as its capital. Major cities include Amritsar, Ludhiana, Jalandhar, Patiala, and Mohali. The five rivers—Sutlej, Beas, Ravi, Chenab, and Jhelum—once flowed through the region, shaping its fertile land. The map highlights key transportation networks, including national highways and railways, connecting Punjab to the rest of India.

The Golden Temple, or Harmandir Sahib, was founded in 1581 by Guru Arjan, the fifth Sikh Guru, in Amritsar. Completed in 1604, it houses the Guru Granth Sahib. Later gilded with gold by Maharaja Ranjit Singh, it remains Sikhism’s holiest shrine.

Bhangra and Gidda are traditional folk dances of Punjab. Bhangra, performed by men, is energetic and lively, celebrating harvest and festivals. Gidda, performed by women, is graceful and expressive, featuring rhythmic claps and humorous verses (boliyan), showcasing Punjab’s rich cultural heritage.

Makki di Roti and Sarson da Saag is a classic Punjabi dish, especially enjoyed in winter. Makki di Roti is a flatbread made from corn flour, while Sarson da Saag is a spiced mustard greens curry. Served with butter and jaggery, it reflects Punjab’s rich culinary tradition.
